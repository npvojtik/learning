Noun Classification System (Gender)

German has three words for 'the': der, das, and die, and German nouns can be divided into three classes according to which of the three words for 'the'they go with.

There are no general rules for classification of the majority of German nouns. For derivative nouns, there are some rules:

  • Nouns ending in -ung always take die, e.g. die Verzeihung (the forgiveness, the pardon)
  • Nouns ending with the diminutive suffixes -lein and -chen always take das, e.g. das FrÃ¤ulein (the miss, waitress), das MÃ¤dchen (the little girl)

In German, the basic sentence structure is an actor-action pattern: somebody doing something. The actor is the subject of the sentence, and in German a pronoun designating the actor always has the nominative form.
