In Spanish, the plural ending depends on the last sound of the singular form:

  • Words ending with a vowel add an -s: mesa => mesas
  • Words ending with a consonant add an -es: hotel => hoteles

Almost all Spanish adjectives change forms for singular and plural the same way nouns do.

Spanish verbs can appear with an accompanying pronoun or noun subject, and often context is the only clue as to what is meant. In cases of ambiguity, or for emphasis, the pronoun can be included.

Usage Notes

  1. Conductor(a) is a word commonly used for "driver"; taxi drivers are also called taxistas.
  2. In Spanish, you cannot omit "that" (que) for subordinate clauses; e.g. in English you can say "I told him to go" but in Spanish you must say "Le dije que vaya."
  3. In Spanish, you use the definite article before someone's title in conversation when talking about them (not to them), e.g. "Sr. Smith, dónde está la Sra. Smith?"
  4. Decir in its command form can also mean "call", as in "No me diga Sr. Molina."
