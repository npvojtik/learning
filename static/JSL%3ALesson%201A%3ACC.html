JSL:Lesson 1A

Core Conversations

Conversation 1

J checks with N on her ability to understand the French material she is looking at.

分かりますわかりますかか✓。
Do you understand?
ええええ、分かりますわかります。
Yes, I do (understand).

Conversation 2

N checks on his colleague's (J's) plans to do some work on the computer.

違います is often used as a denial or negation even without an accompanying いいえ or いや.

今日きょうしますしますねね⸮。
You'll do it today, right?
いや、違いますちがいます。　明日あしたしますしますよ。
No (it's different), I'll do it tomorrow.

Conversation 3

J checks on whether N understood a procedure on the computer that he has just demonstrated.

分かりましたわかりましたかか✓。
Did you understand?
ええええ、分かりましたわかりました。
Yes, I did (understand).

Conversation 4

J checks with N on his assumption that N made up a new computer program.

作りましたつくりましたねね⸮。
You made it, right?
はいはい。昨日きのう作りましたつくりました。
Yes. I made it yesterday.

Conversation 5

N checks with the secretary on whether some work has been completed.

出来ます has a number of different meanings depending on context. In reference to a project it refers to completion. It may also refer to personal ability or capability or possibility. When used of languages, it refers to proficiency.

出来ましたできましたかか✓。
All done?
出来ましたできました。はいはい。
All done. Here you are.

Conversation 6

An office supervisor J checks on her assumption that a part-time student worker N is not coming tomorrow.

明日あす
is a less formal equivalent of
明日あした
.

明日あした来ませんきませんねね⸮。
You're not coming tomorrow, right?
いや、来ますきますよよ✓。
No, I am coming.

Conversation 7

N is invited to join a game of ご during an office break.

します can refer to all kinds of activity.

ちょっと, often with slowed-down articulation, is commonly used as a polite refusal. The literal meaning of the word is 'a bit', 'a small amount.' here it implies 'a bit impossible, inconvenient, or out of the question.' By saying no more than ちょっと, the speaker is less precise and more polite, softening the refusal.

しませんしませんかか✓。
Won't you do it?
ちょっとちょっと…
I'm afraid not.

Conversation 8

J offers his foreign colleague a cup of coffee.

いただきます implies polite, in-group acceptance of something from the out-group, usually the person(s) addressed.

飲みませんのみませんかか✓。
Won't you have (drink) some?
ありがとうありがとうございます。いただきますいただきます。
Thank you. I will (accept).

Conversation 9

J checks on his assumption that his colleague N attended an event advertised on a poster they are looking at.

行きます refers to motion away from the speaker or locations connected with the speaker. In contrast, 来ます refers to motion toward the speaker.

行きましたいきましたねね⸮。
You went, right?
いいえ、行きませんいきませんでした。
No, I didn't (go).

Conversation 10

N apologizes for not having understood a new computer procedure.

すみませんすみません。分かりませんわかりませんでした。
I'm sorry, I didn't understand.
分かりませんわかりませんでしたかか✓。
You didn't (understand)?
ええええ。どうどうもすみませんすみません。
That's right. I'm very sorry.
