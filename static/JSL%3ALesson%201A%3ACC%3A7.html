N is invited to join a game of ご during an office break.

します can refer to all kinds of activity.

ちょっと, often with slowed-down articulation, is commonly used as a polite refusal. The literal meaning of the word is 'a bit', 'a small amount.' here it implies 'a bit impossible, inconvenient, or out of the question.' By saying no more than ちょっと, the speaker is less precise and more polite, softening the refusal.
