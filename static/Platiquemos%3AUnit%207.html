Distance for the purpose of demonstratives can be both physical and in terms of time.

Demonstratives can be used as modifiers or as nominalized forms, e.g. Este libro es más barato que ése. The neuter form nearly always appears nominalized. By convention, in the masculine and feminine nominalized forms, the accent is written over the stressed vowel.

Usage Notes

  1. Auto (from automóvil), coche, and carro are more or less interchangeable; coche and carro are actually old words for wagon and cart, not actual anglicisms
  2. Some count nouns in Spanish translate to mass nouns in English; for example, muebles = furniture, so mueble means something like "a piece of furniture"
