Verb Ser in the Present Tense

Ser is the link of identity (the essential, inherent, or permanent) while estar is the link of association (the causal, accidental, or temporary). For example, "Juan es mi hermano" but "Juan está en el pasillo."

With adjectives, the difference usually expresses a degree, intensity, or sentiment of intrinsicness. For example, "La casa es bonita" expresses that the house is intrinsically pretty, while "La casa está bonita" means that the house is unusually attractive on a specific occasion, e.g. it looks nice(r than usual) today.

There are also some set usages for each verb. For example, the location of people or places is always expressed with estar, regardless of how permanent or impermanent. A few more examples:

  • ¿Cómo está María? – How is Maria?
    ¿Cómo es María? – What is Maria like?
  • ¿Dónde está su marido? – Where is your husband?
    ¿De dónde es su marido? – Where is your husband from?

Usage Notes

  1. When talking about marital status, some places use ser and some places use estar.
